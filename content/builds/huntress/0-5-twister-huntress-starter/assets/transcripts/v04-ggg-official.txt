We mountain hold the spirit dear. But I
seek not just to hold it. I seek to
become its champion. Like the Asmir of
old. I seek to become a spirit walker.
In Return of the Ancients, we're adding
an ascendancy class for the Huntress,
the Spirit Walker. We covered some of
this in our recent content review, but
here we will dig deeper and even
showcase some potential builds.
Most of the ascendency class is devoted
to three aspects of the spirit, vivid,
primal, and wild.
The vivid path conjures stampeding stags
when you attack.
Vivid Stampede gives you extra damage,
area coverage, and shocking
capabilities.
If you want to really focus on the
stags, the Morgan's guidance lets you
create even more stampedes with even
more damage, trampling your enemies into
dust.
The Primal Path, meanwhile, is all about
projectiles.
The owl grants you bounds in the form of
their feathers, which can be spent to
add extra projectiles to your next
skill.
If you'd rather unleash the occasional
barrage, the Mucker's Gift let you store
up and use multiple feathers at once.
And if you're really keen, the owl
feathers work on spells, too.
For those who prefer their animal
companions to be more literal, summon a
wild protector to give your enemies a
bear hug they won't soon forget.
The bear is a powerful companion in its
own right and can also provide
significant utility.
Breaking enemy armor, maming enemies,
violent, intimidating roars, and keeping
itself fit to fight with its large
health pool and life leech.
Best of all, the bear doesn't even take
up your companion slot, so you can keep
your tamed beast alongside you, too.
The Karthur's balance skill can take
your companions damage one step further
by giving them extra damage based on
your weapon.
Spirit walkers who master all three
aspects of the spirit may attract the
favor of the elusive sacred wisps.
The sacred unity passive is free. It
provides enhancements to all three
skills. The stag seek out enemies and
can turn mid stampede.
Our feather empowered skills create a
new type of ground effect.
And the bear protects allies near it.
Taking this node also causes all three
skills to change appearance to reflect
their sacred empowerment.
The spirit walker has a couple of tricks
up her sleeve outside of animal spirits.
Idolatry provides massive bonuses to
more minion focused builds.
And last, but definitely not least, the
natural order passive grants tame beast
the ability to tame bosses.
Yep, all of these.
It probably won't come as a surprise to
hear that these bosses can cut through
packs and bring the pain to their former
friends.
If you'd like the look of the spirit
walker, but need some further
inspiration for what to do with her,
we've put together a couple of endgame
builds to give you ideas, kindly made by
some of our developers.
First up, we have the Stormbbringer.
We have a standard looking twister build
dial to 11, where we utilize whirling
slash and barrage to unleash twisters to
devastate our enemies.
We will use a combination of barrage and
our new notable primal bounty to create
so many twisters it more resembles a
full cyclone.
Allocating Wild Protector, Primal
Bounty, and Vivid Stampede will unlock a
notable that empowers all three, giving
them more utilities and features.
Thanks to Primal Bounty and the Mucker's
gift, we generate a primal owl feather
every few seconds that will be used when
we dodge roll to empower our next
projectile attack, firing more twisters
and increasing their speed.
But now we want to start to empower our
wind ruckus with the elements, gaining
significantly more damage. To do that,
we take vivid stampede, which grants us
a vivid wisp every 10 meters we travel,
which we can do faster by following our
soaring ground.
And then we expend those wisps to
unleash a stampede of stag spirits. Not
only do they help kill enemies, they
also create shock ground.
There you have it. All that combined, we
have our stormb.
And for our second build today, we have
the zookeeper.
This build is for those who'd prefer to
sit back and let your animal army do all
the work. The spirit walker has enlisted
the entire zoo to fight on her behalf
with a whopping six companion skills
active. This is thanks to Silven's
effigy, a new unique scepter coming in
Return of the Ancients that completely
removes the companion cap and also gives
us a big bad alpha wolf that buffs all
our other companions.
We've also got a pack of regular wolves,
three different tamed beasts, including
the mighty Silverfist, and our trusty
beer with our last bits of spirit used
on some skeletal clerics to keep
everyone healthy.
All you have to do is sit back and enjoy
the show and maybe remind your alpha
wolf to summon its spirit friends every
once in a while. This might be a good
time to mention that companion damage
has been buffed across the board.
This build squeezes maximum value out of
spirit walker's idolatry node, even
wearing darkness enthroned to equip more
idols.
Finally, we're going all the way to the
Carthur's Balance and wielding a juicy
spear to grant our companions a lot of
damage.
So, are you ready to embrace the full
power of the spirit? You can do just
that on May 29th when Return of the
Ancients releases. Thanks for watching
and see you all at launch.
