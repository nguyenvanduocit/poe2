These are the official patch notes for Path of Exile 2 — Content Update 0.5.0 "Return of the Ancients", posted by Grinding Gear Games (Stacey_GGG) on 21/05/2026 at [pathofexile2.com/ancients](https://www.pathofexile2.com/ancients), with a supplemental update on 22/05/2026. This is the full changelog, copied line for line for reference — game terms are kept in English. The original file lives in the repo at `data/release-notes/Version_0.5.0.md`. The orientation summary (keeping only the changes big enough to alter how you play) is at [Return of the Ancients](/mechanics/leagues/return-of-the-ancients).

The **Return of the Ancients** expansion brings a new league, a complete overhaul of the entire Path of Exile 2 Endgame, with six new Endgame storylines and 2 new Ascendancy classes, along with a massive amount of other content.

## Runes of Aldur League

- Start over in a fresh economy with the Runes of Aldur league.
- In every area of the Runes of Aldur league, you'll find a **Remnant** that lets you craft an item of your choosing by using **Runic Recipes** inscribed into the slots on the Remnant.
- Depending on the **Runeshapes** used, you'll face an encounter of tough enemies — you must defeat them to claim the item.
- Each additional Runeshape used to craft the item adds more waves of enemies, and each wave adds runic modifiers to the enemies.
- A Remnant starts with any number of slots from 2 to 10 — the higher the slot count the rarer it is, but it allows crafting far rarer items.
- Monsters summoned by the Remnant can drop **Verisium**, a new metal currency.
- Added a new NPC, **Farrow**, with 4 new quests in the campaign that unlock many new crafting features.
- **New Crafting System: Verisium Runeforging.** After unlocking Verisium Runeforging in Act 1, this system lets you add **Runic Ward** to armour by spending Verisium. Armour below level 55 gains Runic Ward with no downside. Armour above that level trades away some of its normal defence to gain Runic Ward.
- **New Defence: Runic Ward.** This defence layer activates when you're at 1 life, letting you keep surviving while Runic Ward takes damage. Runic Ward recovers independently of life.
- Added 13 new **Alloy** currencies — unlocked through Remnant encounters after completing Farrow's quest in Act 2. These items add new crafted modifiers to items by replacing an existing modifier, similar to Perfect Essence.
- After unlocking **Unique Verisium Runeforging** in Act 3, you can upgrade the base type of Unique Weapons and Armour dropped below level 55. An upgraded Unique Weapon will have higher damage, and upgraded Unique Armour will have higher base defence along with Runic Ward — making them more competitive at high level. Unique Armour above level 55 can still be Runeforged to adjust its defence to gain Runic Ward, in exchange for reduced other defences just like base types.
- Unique Verisium Runeforging has additional special properties when used on a Kalguuran Unique.
- Added 13 **Ancient Runes** — unlocked for crafting through Remnant encounters after completing Farrow's quest in Act 4. These runes have strong bonuses specific to each weapon type.
- Added 13 **Mythical** runes for the early game — crafted from Remnant encounters, providing a significant boost for level 15+ characters.
- Added 3 **Flux** — crafted from the Remnant to convert a resistance on an item from one element to another.
- Added 15 **Meta crafting runes** — crafted from the Remnant.
- Added more than 60 runes that can be made by destroying a unique to create a rune carrying part of its properties.
- Added more than 15 **Runic Ward Runes** — crafted from the Remnant, adding or adjusting properties related to Runic Ward.
- Added 21 **Kalguuran Skills** craftable from the Remnant: Animus Exchange, Animus Splinters, Bitter Dead, Conductive Runes, Detonate Living, Eternal March, Explosive Transmutation, Fragments of the Past, Frostflame Nova, Grim Pillars, Hollow Shell, Leylines, Powered by Verisium, Refutation, Remnants of Kalguur, Repulsion, Runic Reprieve, Skyfall, Triskelion Cascade, Verisium Manifestations, and Voltaic Barrier.
- Added 8 **Kalguuran Supports** craftable from the Remnant: Concussive Runes, Fist of Kalguur, Healing Runes, Kalguuran Conviction, Runeforged Blades, Runic Extraction, Runic Infusion, and Scouring Flame.
- The Expedition Remnant has been changed into the Remnant of the Runes of Aldur league.
- **New Endgame Story:** Set out on an expedition with Farrow to explore the Ocean as you investigate the tombs of the fallen Kalguuran and challenge them for extraordinary rewards. Seek out the impact crater of a fallen star — a meteorite of pure Verisium — where you hope to forge items of unmatched power… if you can defeat what waits there.
- There is a new hub area, **"The Ruins of Kingsmarch"**, for Runes of Aldur, located to the south-east of your starting position on the Atlas.
- The **Logbook** mechanic has been changed into the **Ocean Exploring** mechanic. Using a logbook now reveals an ocean region with many islands to explore.
- Many new underground areas have been added, explorable during expeditions.
- Many new islands have been added with new **Grand Expeditions** (previously called logbooks).
- Medved, Vorana and Uhtred join Olroth as 4 Faction Leaders fightable on the new islands you explore in the Ocean.
- Defeating Olroth gives you a key used to access the new Pinnacle Boss of Runes of Aldur.
- The Runes of Aldur challenge league includes the first set of challenges in Path of Exile 2. Receive the pieces of the **Knight of Aldur** armour set as you complete 2, 4, 6 and 8 challenges in Runes of Aldur. These challenge rewards are only obtainable in this league.
- From the 1st challenge onward, and with each subsequent challenge, you'll receive pieces of the **Runes of Aldur Totem** decoration to display in your hideout. This Totem permanently shows how many Runes of Aldur challenges you completed in the league.
- All old characters from Early Access remain, but have been granted a one-time free passive tree refund due to the changes. We highly encourage you to join the new league to fully experience the changes in 0.5.0.
- With 0.5.0, there are Standard, Hardcore and Solo Self-Found versions of the Runes of Aldur challenge league. They share the same core mechanics and items, and you can create private league versions of these leagues.
- The Hardcore version of Runes of Aldur is parented to the corresponding Standard version — meaning if you die in Hardcore Runes of Aldur, you can continue in the Standard Runes of Aldur league.

## Endgame

- The Atlas now has fixed points of interest at specific locations, giving you clear objectives that are easy to find.
- Every league mechanic on the Atlas now has a quest that introduces the mechanic and guides you through its stages all the way to the Pinnacle Boss.
- Every Pinnacle Boss now has a quest version accessible in a deterministic way, alongside a repeatable non-quest version that brings a greater challenge.
- Added 30 new Endgame Map Areas.

## Endgame Overhaul — Origins of Divinity

- **New Endgame Storyline:** The Cataclysm of the Beast has triggered an ancient protocol, designed to hunt down and purge the corruption should it ever take root to this extent. Cross the Atlas to enter the Fortress and activate the Gates. Take the keys to reach the ancient Precursor weapons and disable them.
- When you complete the first tower, a **Fortress** will rise from the ground.
- Maps inside the Fortress grant one or more passive points for the **Atlas Passive Tree**. This mechanic completely replaces the previous way of getting Atlas Points.
- The Atlas Tree has been significantly expanded with over 300 nodes. After completing all the maps inside the Fortress, you'll have enough passive points to allocate the entire Atlas Tree.
- Because the whole atlas tree can be fully allocated, there's no longer a need to allow respec. Multi-choice nodes on the tree can be swapped between options at any time.
- Many maps in the Fortress have an **Ancient Modifier** — adding or altering the map's content. Any map with a modifier that adds secondary content also implicitly adds that content to the map.
- Added more than 40 Ancient Modifiers with a chance to appear on any map outside the Fortress.
- Added three **Gateway maps** with two new bosses, used to access different parts of the Fortress.
- Added new **Precursor Tower Maps** outside the Fortress.
- The Burning Monolith along with the Arbiter of Ash have been moved inside the Fortress.
- The quest version of the Crisis fragments has been added to two Enigma rooms inside the Fortress.
- Added two new **Citadel maps** with two new bosses that drop keys for the new Pinnacle Boss.
- Added Pinnacle Boss **Arbiter of Divinity**.
- The maps in the Fortress can also be completed an alternative way: killing the Arbiter of Divinity 5 times automatically completes different parts of the Fortress in order to gain Atlas Tree points.

## Masters of the Atlas

- Alongside the atlas tree, a new system called **Masters of the Atlas** introduces an Ascendancy-style progression for the endgame. Players can ally with different masters — like Jado of the Order of the Djinn or the beast hunter Hilda — each master providing unique asymmetric bonuses that can be swapped between maps freely, opening up build-defining bonuses.
- Each master has 12 nodes, of which 4 can be chosen at a time. You can change your selection at any time.
- All three masters can be allocated at once, with a quick-select button to switch between masters before running a map.
- Unlock each row of 3 nodes by performing missions for the masters.
- **New Atlas Master: Doryani's Science** — questline unlocked by clearing a corruption nexus.
- **New Atlas Master: Hilda's Hunting** — questline unlocked by visiting Hilda's Campsite, slightly to the south-west of your starting position on the atlas.
- **New Atlas Master: Jado's Spycraft** — questline unlocked by completing an anomaly map near your starting position.

## Delirium

- **New Storyline:** An ancient Trickster God has been freed, sowing madness across Wraeclast. Do you dare hunt down its origin and face your darkest fear?
- There is a new hub area, **"The Withered Willow"**, for Delirium, located to the south-west of your starting position on the Atlas. The maps around this position all contain Delirium and grant points for the Delirium Atlas Passive Tree.
- The Delirium Atlas Passive Tree has been completely reworked with many new nodes and changed old nodes.
- After breaking a Delirium mirror, there's now a progress bar showing how far you've gone into the mist and how much longer until the mist disperses. The start of the Delirium bar represents your initial position at the Delirium mirror, and the end of the bar represents the map boss's position.
- There's now a new set of **Delirium Encounters** displayed on the Delirium bar. Reaching a certain depth causes colored mirror shards to appear. Entering the mirror shards breaks them, triggering new encounters.
- The map boss's direction can be observed through the mist effect. Moving toward the mist always leads you to the map boss.
- The map boss in Delirium will always be at 100% delirious.
- The amount of damage and life bonus that Delirium grants monsters has been reduced.
- Added 5 new Elite Delirium Monsters.
- Added a new sub-area: **Loathsome Mire**.
- Loathsome Mire drops two new Amulet base types — granting two instilled notables, in exchange for -1 suffix or -1 prefix.
- **Liquid Emotion** can now be used to craft additional mods on Jewels. The mechanic is similar to a greater essence — each type has a specific set of mods that replaces a random existing mod on the item.
- Completing a Delirium Mirror now has a chance to spawn a **Grand Mirror** on the Atlas at a nearby map. The Grand mirror creates a copy of the map boss in the affected map. Killing both map bosses unlocks a new atlas mechanic: **The Trial of Madness**.
- After activating the Trial of Madness, mist will spread from a map you choose on the Atlas — including a locked Simulacrum. While in the mist, the entire map is Delirious, starting at 10%. Killing Rare monsters and the Map boss increases the Delirious level of all maps in the mist. Reaching 100% unlocks the Simulacrum. The Delirious level can keep increasing up to 200%.
- Rare and Unique monsters in a Delirious map have a new set of Delirious modifiers, granting them strange abilities.
- The map boss of a Delirious map can also drop a new set of 3 **Potent emotions** — used to craft new powerful modifiers on Jewels that the normal mod pool doesn't have. These emotions can also be used to instill a new set of 16 **Passive Tree Notables** not otherwise available on the normal passive tree.
- The Simulacrum has been updated and is now a 7-wave encounter.
- Completing a Simulacrum now gives you a key to face the new Delirium Pinnacle Boss.
- There's a new set of 10 **Ancient Emotions** that can drop if unlocked via the Atlas Tree — used to craft **Timelost Jewels**. There are also 3 **Ancient Potent emotions** used to craft new powerful modifiers on Timelost Jewels not in the normal mod pool.
- Some non-amulet Delirium Uniques now drop with the **Raven-Touched** modifier, allowing them to be instilled.

## Breach

- **New Breach Storyline:** Journey to the Monastery of the Keepers — the sacred hall of the Keepers of the Flame — and discover The Genesis Tree, stolen from the enemy then gifted to the Keepers by the Dreamer himself.
- There is a new Hub Area, **"The Monastery of the Keepers"**, to the south of your starting position on the atlas. The maps around this position all contain Breach encounters. Completing these maps grants you points for the Breach Atlas Tree.
- The Breach Atlas Passive Tree has been completely reworked with many new nodes and changed old nodes.
- Starting a breach now displays a progress bar showing how much longer until the breach closes, as well as how much killing monsters is extending the Breach's lifespan.
- Reaching 100% on this bar activates a new mechanic: **A Stabilised Breach**. A stabilised breach spawns new challenges at its starting point.
- Added a new Boss for the Stabilised Breach: **Vruun, Marshal of Xesht**.
- Added a new Crafting System: **The Genesis Tree**.
- The Genesis Tree lets you consume **Wombgift** and **Hiveblood** to create Rings, Amulets, Belts and Currency.
- Nodes on the Genesis Tree can be allocated to influence which item gets crafted.
- New nodes are unlocked by using Wombgift.
- Added 6 new Ring base types craftable only from the Genesis Tree.
- Added 4 new Amulet base types craftable only from the Genesis Tree.
- Added 4 new Belt base types craftable only from the Genesis Tree.
- Added a new set of Caster and Minion mods craftable onto Rings and Belts using the Genesis Tree.
- **Catalysts** no longer drop from monsters — they're now only obtainable from the Genesis Tree.
- Added a new set of 12 Catalysts that can add new quality modifiers to Jewels.
- Killing monsters in Breach now drops Hiveblood, Wombgift and Breachstone Splinters.
- A Breachstone splinter turns into a special wombgift when fully stacked. This item can be submitted at the Genesis Tree to create a Breachstone.
- New **Breach Domains** now spawn on the Atlas. Using a Breachstone reveals the maps inside the domain, allowing you to enter.
- Maps inside a breach domain contain new encounters: **Breach Hive**, **Sky Hive** and **Sky Fortress**.
- Breach Hive has a series of walls that can be burned down by walking through them. Each wall broken increases the number of monsters in the encounter — the encounter can be started from the center.
- Sky Hive is a new area where the Breachlords have built a fortress you need to assault. The sky hive contains a challenging multi-wave encounter in which you need to protect Ailith while she closes the domain. Ailith gains a series of abilities that can be activated during the encounter with various effects.
- The Fortress is a new area with a new boss pair, **Tul** and **Esh**. Defeating Tul and Esh gives you a key to access the existing Breach Pinnacle Boss.

## Ritual

- **New Storyline:** Aoife lingers as a restless spirit, bound to the Wildwood and seeking someone who can answer her call. The King in the Mists has taken her body, leaving her spirit adrift, unanchored and unable to pass on. She believes only by reclaiming what was lost can she be made whole again.
- There is a new Hub Area, **"Caer Tarth"**, to the west of your starting position on the atlas. The maps around this position all contain Ritual encounters. Completing these maps grants you points for the Ritual Atlas Tree.
- The Ritual Atlas Passive Tree has been completely reworked with many new nodes and changed old nodes.
- After completing a Ritual Altar encounter in a Map, a swarm of locusts will point the way to the next Altar.
- Every item on the Endgame Ritual Reward screen is now a Unique or Omen.
- Any tribute not spent on rewards can now be sacrificed to earn an **Audience with the King**.
- Killing the King in the Mists now drops a new key, **The Head of the King**. This key is used at Caer Tarth to start the **Rite of the Nameless**.
- The Rite of the Nameless is a new atlas mechanic in which you choose a chain of 5 maps to complete a single continuous ritual. Monsters from each ritual, including the map boss, reappear in each map of the chain, forming an extremely difficult encounter. Each map's Unique boss only appears in that map's final ritual.
- When choosing maps for the Rite of the Nameless, each map after the first one has an additional modifier affecting the difficulty and rewards of the rituals in the Rite.
- Each map in the Rite of the Nameless grants a piece of the key used to access the Ritual Pinnacle Boss.
- Added a new Boss: **The Queen in the Mists**, opened after allocating a specific node on the Atlas Tree. This boss can drop three new **Corrupted Idols**.
- Freythorn Ritual now never displays deferred items, to ensure items aren't lost if you return to this area on a higher-level character.

## Fate of the Vaal Moves Into Core

- **Fate of the Vaal** has been added to the Core Game. You first encounter a series of 6 **Ancient Beacons** in Act 3 — charge them to take an **Energised Crystal** and open a portal into the Vaal Ruins. You'll encounter a second series of 6 Ancient Beacons in the Interludes.
- There is now an Atlas Passive Tree for the Fate of the Vaal league mechanic.
- **Atziri's Temple** can now be found on the atlas to the north-east of the starting position, in the city of Lira Vaal. Maps inside the city of Lira Vaal always have an Energised Crystal and grant points for the Fate of the Vaal Atlas Passive Tree.
- There are also new **Temple Precursor Tablets** that guarantee a Vaal Beacon in maps if you want to focus on this league.
- It's no longer possible to prevent a room from being removed from the temple. Rooms that must be removed but have another connection turn into simple Path rooms. This prevents the "snake" strategy.
- The number of rooms in the temple at the start has been increased to reach Atziri faster.
- Rooms can now be upgraded to Tier 4 after unlocking this ability on the Temple Atlas Tree — allowing deeper specialization into each room type and encouraging more interesting temple layouts.
- The Reward Rooms granted from the console after defeating the Architect have been significantly improved, with more types to unlock along with upgrades to the rewards of all existing rooms.
- A Restricted Room in Atziri's Temple that is reachable will always destabilise when you leave the Temple.
- The **Atziri's Temple Medallion** now has a drop rate that scales with the number of paths in the Temple, in addition to the other rooms (previously only by the other rooms).
- Room-based Medallions can no longer be used inside an active Temple.
- Room-based Medallions now display upgrade information on hover.
- Medallions now output a descriptive error message when they can't be placed.
- The default maximum number of Energised Crystals stored has been increased to 60, and the maximum number of Medallions increased to 6. Medallions that increase the number of stored Energised Crystals or Medallions are no longer necessary.
- The Temple's monster level now also factors in the level of the areas where the crystals used to open it were obtained.
- The Corruption Chamber Temple Bonus has been updated to add Monster Modifiers Chance, instead of Additional Monster Modifiers.
- Portals created in the Temple of Atziri now create a corresponding portal in the original Vaal Ruins for easier access.
- Vaal Beacons now display a dimmed icon on the Minimap when completed.
- There are now 4 types of Infuser. The current Vaal Infuser becomes the **Vaal Armourer's Infuser**, applying to Armour.
- The **Vaal Blacksmith's Infuser** applies to Martial Weapons; the **Vaal Arcanist's Infuser** applies to Wands, Staves and Sceptres.
- The **Vaal Catalysing Infuser** applies to Jewellery.
- Every Infuser can only be used on items at 20% Quality or higher.
- Fate of the Vaal Currency Items now have a maximum stack size of 5,000 when stored in a wildcard slot of the Currency Stash Tab.
- Some results of using a **Vaal Cultivation Orb** on a Unique Item have been updated:
- The Unique Staff **Atziri's Rule** can now roll 10-20% increased Life Cost Efficiency (previously 20-40%).
- The Unique Body Armour **Atziri's Splendour** can now roll +66-100 to Maximum Energy Shield (previously +100-200).
- The Unique Body Armour **The Covenant** can now roll 10-25% increased Life Cost Efficiency (previously 25-50%).
- The Unique Gloves **Hateforge** can now roll +16-30 maximum Rage if you've used a Skill that Requires Glory in the past 20 seconds (previously +8-12 maximum Rage per Skill that requires Glory used in the past 6 seconds, up to 5 times).
- The Unique Focus **Rathpith Globe** can now roll 8-15% increased Life Cost Efficiency (previously 12-20%).
- The Unique Gloves **Shackles of the Wretched** can no longer roll "Elemental Ailments other than Freeze you inflict are Reflected to you" — instead it can now roll 10-15% increased Damage per Curse on you.
- The Unique Helmet **The Vertex** can no longer roll +2-4 to Level of All Skill Gems — instead it can now roll +3-5 to Level of All Curse Skill Gems.

## Abyss

- There are now large Abyss cracks on the atlas. Completing an Atlas Abyss closes them and always gives you an Abyssal Depths with a boss fight.
- The Abyss Atlas Tree has been reworked with many new nodes and changed old nodes.
- **Kulemak's Invitation** is now always allocated for the Map owner.
- Abyss Omens no longer drop in areas below level 65.
- Modifiers affecting the number of chests found now apply to Chests found in the Abyss. They also apply to the chance to find an Abyssal Depths, so the chance to find an Abyssal Depths doesn't decrease when there are more chests in the area.

- The **Lithomantic Rune** from the Abyss Monster modifier Lithomantic Runes now activates after a longer delay.

- The Expedition league has been temporarily disabled on the Standard leagues due to its integration with Runes of Aldur. It will be reintegrated into Standard after Runes of Aldur ends.
- The **Recombinator** has been disabled. The **Omen of Recombination** has been removed. Existing Omens of Recombination will be deleted on login.
- The Expedition Precursor Tablet **Forgotten By Time** has temporarily stopped dropping.
- Expedition Explosives now wait to detonate until most of the monsters unearthed by a previous Explosive have been killed.

## Other Endgame Changes

- With the introduction of the new endgame storyline, the Atlas has been reset. Existing Tablets and waystones still work so you can continue your progress as usual, but to gain points for the Atlas Tree you need to do the Origins of Divinity storyline.
- Added search support for the Endgame Atlas Map.
- You can now zoom out slightly more on the Atlas screen.
- There are now two versions of each pinnacle boss: a Quest version and an Infinite Farm version. As a result, the Primary, Secondary and Tertiary Calamity Fragments are no longer obtainable.
- Tablets of the same type can now be combined to increase the amount of league content spawned. The specifics differ per content type — sometimes adding more tablets increases the size of a single encounter, sometimes it increases the number of encounters spawned.
- Each empty tablet slot now contributes to the amount of random (non-tablet) league content spawned in the area. This means if every tablet slot is full, you only see league content from the tablets in use. The purpose of this change is to ensure you don't need to complete the atlas trees of leagues you don't intend to run.
- Waystones now have to be identified before they can be activated in the Map Device.
- You can now use the **Orb of Chance** on Tablets.
- Some items and content now require you to specialize via the Atlas Passive Skill tree, including Exceptional Items, Fracturing Orbs, and Basic and Overseer precursor tablets.
- Tablets can no longer be used on maps that can't have extra content (such as Unique Maps).
- League-specific tablets no longer drop from non-Map areas, such as Simulacrum, Abyssal Depths or Twisted Domain.
- Reduced the base chances for Essence, Azmeri Spirit, Shrine, Strongbox, Summoning Circle and Rogue Exile to appear in Maps, due to the much larger increases now available on the Atlas Tree.
- The Atlas Passive referencing maps with a Powerful Map Boss now works with the Overseer Precursor Tablet and other sources of Powerful Map Boss.
- Adjusted the values of general Precursor Tablet Modifiers to be similar to the same type of bonus granted by Waystone Modifiers. Also reduced the roll variance of these modifiers.
- The following Modifiers can no longer roll on Waystones: Baron's, Beastly, Brambled, Doryani's, Enervating, Faridun's, Hallowed, of Nemeses, Perennial's, Rusted, and Sacrificial.
- Whether a Map Modifier is a Suffix or Prefix has been adjusted: in general, Prefixes are Modifiers that affect monster output aimed at the player, while Suffixes are Modifiers that affect the player or the monster's defence.
- The following Suffix Modifiers are now Prefixes: Of Destruction, of Fleeting, of Impact, of Penetration, of Puncturing, of Shattering, of Splitting, and of Venom.
- The following Prefix Modifiers are now Suffixes: Buffering, Enduring, Enfeebling, Eroding, Evasive, Flaming, Hexwarded, Shocking, Sleet, and Slowing.
- The Waystone Modifier **of Overpowering** no longer makes Monsters have increased Freeze Buildup, Shock Chance, or inflict increased Flammability Magnitude. It now makes Monsters have increased Elemental Ailment Application.
- The bonuses of Waystone Modifiers have been updated: they now provide more Monster Effectiveness, Pack Size, Item Rarity, Monster Rarity, or Waystone Drop Chance — depending on the modifier. These Modifiers are multiplicative with other sources, such as those on the Atlas Passive Tree.
- Modifiers affecting Pack Size now also provide a chance for an additional Rare Monster to appear in Rare Monster packs.
- **Monster Rarity** is a new stat affecting the chance for magic and rare monsters to appear, along with the chance for additional modifiers on Rare Monsters.
- Map modifiers from sources that increase the number of Rare Monsters or magic Monsters have been updated to only show the number in the map's header, not mixed in among the explicit modifiers. This is not a functional change.
- Halved the Quantity of Items Dropped and Experience Gain bonuses provided by increased Monster Effectiveness (such as from Atziri's Temple).
- The functionality of the Omen of Chaotic Rarity, Omen of Chaotic Quantity and Omen of Chaotic Monsters has been inverted. Instead of guaranteeing the new modifier is of that type, they now prevent a modifier of that type.
- Added a new **Omen of Chaotic Effectiveness**. While this item is active in your inventory, your next Chaos Orb will replace all Modifiers on a Waystone with Modifiers that don't grant Monster Effectiveness.
- Up to 3 of the above Omens can be used at once.
- Halved the "Damage as Extra" buff granted by the following shrines: Freezing Shrine, Gloom Shrine, Meteoric Shrine and Tempest Shrine.
- Completing fixed locations on the Atlas is now shared with party members.
- With the addition of more varied content on the Atlas Map, the size of content indicators has been increased by 50%.
- Waystones receive new 2D artwork.

## Replaying the Campaign

- To improve the time experienced players spend replaying the game each new league, elements have been added in many Campaign areas that show you the way — if you know what to look for.
- Some areas in the game have been shortened.
- Removed the second dreadnaught area, "The Dreadnaught Vanguard". The Act 2 boss is now located in "The Dreadnaught".
- The area order in Act 3 has been rearranged slightly for smoother progression.
- The levers in the Waterways have turned into pressure pads you simply step over, and the end of the level has been pre-drained of water.
- Monster density in the second half of the campaign has been reduced, especially in the Interludes, to pass through these areas faster.

## Player Changes

- Bleed damage on the player no longer increases while the player is moving. This change does not affect bleed the player inflicts on monsters.
- Your maximum Runic Ward is now added to your starting Honour when starting a Trial of the Sekhemas.
- The keyword **"Defences"** is no longer used. Places that used the word "Defences" now explicitly reference "Armour, Evasion and Energy Shield" to clarify that these modifiers don't apply to Runic Ward, Resistance, Block or other forms of protection. This is purely a descriptive change.
- Only one Leech instance per resource type (Life, Mana, or Energy Shield) applies at a time. When multiple Leech instances are active, only the instance with the highest recovery rate recovers for you until it expires, after which the next highest-recovery-rate instance applies.
- There is now a limit on the maximum damage a hit counts for Leech. Hits dealing under 40,000 total damage are unaffected, but if a hit deals more than 40,000 total damage, it counts as dealing only 40,000 damage for the purposes of calculating Leech. The value of each damage type in the hit is scaled down evenly to reach this limit. This means extremely high-damage hits stop improving Leech past a certain point, but increasing % leech will always increase the amount of resource you receive.
- The chance to Deflect formula has been adjusted to scale better with investment in Deflection Rating, with a cap of 95% chance to Deflect (similar to the 95% chance to Evade cap).
- The new chance to Deflect = 150*(1 - A/(A + 0.12*D)), where A = the attacker's accuracy rating and D = the defender's deflection rating. This formula creates a linear curve, rewarding higher investment more.
- Parry, Shield Block and Resonating Shield no longer delay Heavy Stun buildup longer than intended.
- Melee Attacks can now only apply Splash Damage once per damage area. Some Attacks create multiple damage areas when used — for example Whirling Assault creates multiple damage areas around you as you move.

## Patch Notes Update (22/05/2026)

GGG updated two patch note entries on 22/05/2026:

- **Unique Verisium Runeforging:** After unlocking Unique Verisium Runeforging in Act 3, you can upgrade the base type of Unique Weapons and Armour dropped below level 55. An upgraded Unique Weapon will have higher damage, and upgraded Unique Armour will have higher base defence along with Runic Ward — making them more competitive at high level. Unique Armour above level 55 can still be Runeforged to adjust its defence to gain Runic Ward, in exchange for reduced other defences just like the base type.
- **Ghost Dance:** The interval between gaining Ghost Shrouds is no longer a duration. Now has "Modifiers to Cooldown Recovery Rate also apply to Ghost Shroud gain frequency". Also has "When Hit, lose a Ghost Shroud" and "Regenerate Energy Shield equal to 2% of your Evasion Rating per second if you have lost a Ghost Shroud Recently". Now gains a Ghost Shroud every 11.7-10.1 seconds at Gem level 4-20 (previously 7.6-6.1).
