# Return of the Ancients

0.5.0 is the last major patch for Path of Exile 2 before 1.0, launching on **29/05/2026**, with the patch notes dropping eight days ahead. This is not a league that bolts on a few mechanics — the entire Endgame is rewritten: the Atlas is reset, six new endgame storylines, two new ascendancies (Martial Artist for Monk, Spirit Walker for Huntress), and for the first time Path of Exile 2 has a challenge system. This note is an orientation map — filtering 789 lines of changelog down to the changes big enough to alter how you play a character. Every verbatim line (skill scaling, unique mods, ascendancy nodes) is looked up in the [Patch Notes 0.5.0](/mechanics/leagues/0-5-0-patch-notes); here I keep only what's worth locking a decision on before touching the league.

## Runes of Aldur — The League And The Verisium Crafting System

The league revolves around the **Remnant** — an object scattered through each area, each one with anywhere from 2 to 10 slots for inscribing **Runic Recipes**. The more slots, the rarer it is and the more precious the item it can craft, but each added **Runeshape** doesn't just define the item you receive — it also adds a wave of enemies carrying runic modifiers. This is crafting with risk: to get a good item you have to win a harder encounter, not click an orb and pray. The monsters summoned by the Remnant drop **Verisium** — a new metal currency, the foundation of the entire crafting system.

The mechanic that ripples across the whole patch is **Runic Ward** — a completely new defence layer. Unlike Energy Shield (which sits in front of the life pool), Runic Ward only activates when the character drops to 1 life, letting you survive longer while Runic Ward takes damage, and it recovers independently of life. The **Verisium Runeforging** system (unlocked in Act 1) consumes Verisium to attach Runic Ward to armour: armour below level 55 gets it free, armour above that threshold trades away part of its normal defence to gain Runic Ward. The patch notes make it explicit that the keyword "Defences" now refers only to Armour/Evasion/Energy Shield — it **doesn't** touch Runic Ward, so this is a separate layer that needs separate stats to scale.

The new NPC **Farrow** gradually opens the crafting tiers through four quests across the campaign. Act 1 opens Verisium Runeforging; Act 2 opens 13 **Alloys** (replacing an existing modifier, like Perfect Essence); Act 3 opens **Unique Verisium Runeforging** (upgrading the base type of a Unique Weapon/Armour dropped below level 55 — the weapon gets higher damage, the armour gets higher base defence plus Runic Ward, with special properties when used on a Kalguuran Unique); Act 4 opens 13 **Ancient Runes** specific to weapon types. On top of that, the Remnant can craft 13 **Mythical** runes for level 15+ characters, 3 **Flux** to swap a resistance's element, 15 **Meta crafting runes**, more than 60 runes made by destroying a unique to keep part of its properties, and more than 15 **Runic Ward Runes** that adjust Runic Ward stats directly. The most notable for gameplay: **21 Kalguuran Skills and 8 Kalguuran Supports** craftable straight from the Remnant — the first time skills/supports are drops from a league mechanic rather than normal gems, so which one to craft early is a week-one build decision.

The old Logbook mechanic is replaced by **Ocean Exploring** — using a logbook now reveals an ocean region full of islands, each island being a Grand Expedition or a Faction Leader to fight. Medved, Vorana and Uhtred join Olroth to form four Faction Leaders; defeating Olroth gives the key to the league's Pinnacle Boss. The new hub area is **The Ruins of Kingsmarch**, to the south-east of the starting point on the Atlas.

On the long-term side: **Path of Exile 2 has challenges for the first time**. Completing 2/4/6/8 Runes of Aldur challenges grants the pieces of the **Knight of Aldur** armour set — a reward that only exists in this league, unobtainable afterward. From the first challenge onward, each challenge grants a piece of the **Runes of Aldur Totem** that permanently records how many challenges you completed, in your hideout. Old characters from Early Access remain and get a one-time free passive tree refund, but the full weight of the 0.5.0 changes is only felt by creating a new character in the league.

## Origins of Divinity — The Endgame Rewritten

This is the heaviest overhaul. The **Origins of Divinity** storyline leads the player into a **Fortress** that rises from the ground after completing the first tower. The crux: **maps inside the Fortress are the ONLY source of Atlas Passive Points** — the old way of getting points is dropped entirely. The Atlas Tree expands to over 300 nodes, and because completing the whole Fortress gives enough points to allocate the entire tree, GGG drops respec altogether — it's unnecessary when every node can be turned on, and multi-choice nodes can be swapped back and forth at any time.

The consequence: the Atlas is no longer the problem of "where do I spend points for optimization" but rather "how far have I farmed the Fortress". Maps inside the Fortress carry an **Ancient Modifier** that adds or alters map content, and more than 40 of these modifiers can also appear on normal maps outside the Fortress. The path into the Fortress goes through three **Gateway maps** with two new bosses; the Burning Monolith and Arbiter of Ash have moved inside; two new **Citadel maps** drop keys for the Pinnacle Boss. The new Pinnacle Boss is the **Arbiter of Divinity**, and there's a shortcut: instead of farming all the Fortress maps, killing the Arbiter of Divinity five times automatically completes the Fortress sections to gain points. The patch adds 30 new Endgame Map Areas and a new stat — **Monster Rarity** — affecting the chance of magic/rare monsters appearing along with the number of modifiers on rares.

A few other Atlas structural changes worth remembering: the Atlas now has fixed points of interest that are easy to find (completion is shared with the party), every league mechanic has a quest leading all the way to its pinnacle, and each pinnacle boss splits into two versions — a deterministically-accessible Quest version and a harder repeatable Infinite Farm version. Because of this split, the Calamity Fragment (Primary/Secondary/Tertiary) no longer exists.

## Masters of the Atlas

Alongside the Atlas Tree is an ascendancy-style progression system for the endgame. Three **Masters** — Doryani's Science, Hilda's Hunting, Jado's Spycraft — each have 12 nodes, you pick 4 at once, and all three can be allocated in parallel. The nice part is that node choices change freely before each map via a quick-select button, so this is a "shape the build per map" layer rather than a hard commitment. You unlock each row of 3 nodes by doing a master's missions, and each questline opens its own way: clearing a corruption nexus opens Doryani, visiting a campsite opens Hilda, completing an anomaly map opens Jado.

## Delirium, Breach, Ritual — New Storylines And Atlas Trees

The three old coreified leagues are all elevated into full storylines with their own hub area and a completely rewritten Atlas Passive Tree, each with a new endgame tier on top.

:wiki-link{url="https://www.poe2wiki.net/wiki/Delirium"} (hub **The Withered Willow**) now has a progress bar showing your depth in the mist and the time remaining; the mist direction always points toward the map boss, which is always at 100% delirious. Completing the Delirium Mirror has a chance to spawn a **Grand Mirror** that creates a copy of the map boss — killing both unlocks **The Trial of Madness**, blanketing a whole Atlas region in mist and pushing every map up to a maximum 200% Delirious. The Simulacrum becomes a 7-wave encounter leading to the new Delirium Pinnacle Boss, and Liquid Emotion now crafts additional mods onto Jewels. Important for the player: the damage and life bonuses Delirium grants monsters have been reduced — Delirium is less dangerous than before.

:wiki-link{url="https://www.poe2wiki.net/wiki/Breach"} (hub **The Monastery of the Keepers**) adds a time counter; pushing it to 100% activates a **Stabilised Breach** with the new boss Vruun, Marshal of Xesht. Accompanying it is the **The Genesis Tree** crafting system — consuming Wombgift and Hiveblood to create Rings, Amulets, Belts with new base types found only here, and allocating tree nodes to choose which item gets crafted. An easy-to-miss but high-impact economic change: **Catalysts no longer drop from monsters, they're only obtainable from the Genesis Tree**, along with 12 new Catalysts adding quality modifiers to Jewels. Inside the Breach Domain there's also the Sky Hive (protect Ailith while she closes the domain) and the Sky Fortress with the boss pair Tul and Esh.

:wiki-link{url="https://www.poe2wiki.net/wiki/Ritual"} (hub **Caer Tarth**) lets a swarm of locusts point the way to the next Altar. Every reward on the Endgame Ritual screen is now only a Unique or Omen, with surplus tribute sacrificed to earn an Audience with the King. Killing the King in the Mists drops **The Head of the King**, used at Caer Tarth to start the **Rite of the Nameless** — choosing a chain of 5 maps as one continuous ritual, where the monsters from each ritual (including the map boss) reappear in the later maps, creating an extremely dense pile-up encounter. The new boss Queen in the Mists drops three Corrupted Idols.

## Fate of the Vaal, Abyss And Atlas-wide Changes

**Fate of the Vaal** is moved straight into the Core Game — you encounter a series of 6 Ancient Beacons right in Act 3, charge them up to take an Energised Crystal that opens a portal into the Vaal Ruins — and it also has its own Atlas Passive Tree. Atziri's Temple sits in the city of Lira Vaal to the north-east of the starting point; the Vaal Infuser now splits into four types by gear group (Armourer/Blacksmith/Arcanist/Catalysing), the storage limit increases to 60 Energised Crystals and 6 Medallions. **Abyss** now has large cracks on the Atlas — completing one always gives an Abyssal Depths with a boss, the tree is reworked, and Kulemak's Invitation is always allocated for the map owner.

A series of changes that cut across every league reshape the Atlas rhythm. Tablets of the same type now stack to increase content, and each empty tablet slot contributes to random content — meaning you don't need to complete the atlas trees of leagues you don't intend to run. **Waystones must be identified before they can be activated in the Map Device** — an extra step every map. The **Orb of Chance** can now be used on Tablets. And three familiar Omens are inverted: the Omen of Chaotic Rarity, Quantity, and Monsters now **prevent** that type of modifier instead of guaranteeing it — along with a new Omen of Chaotic Effectiveness that prevents Monster Effectiveness modifiers; up to 3 can be used at once. Anyone used to the old meaning who clicks by habit will get the opposite result.

## Crafting Economy — Expedition Off, Recombinator Removed, Currency Reshuffle

The Expedition league is temporarily disabled on the Standard leagues due to the integration of Runes of Aldur (it'll be returned after the league ends). Following that, the **Recombinator is disabled and the Omen of Recombination is removed** — existing ones are deleted on login. This is a major crafting economy change: gear archetypes that relied on recombination for top-end crafting lose their main tool, forcing a shift to the new league's Alloys/Ancient Runes/Genesis Tree.

The currency tiers are also reshuffled. Greater and Perfect currency are a bit rarer, with Transmutation and Augmentation notably rarer; to compensate, the Greater Orb of Transmutation and Augmentation lower the minimum Modifier Level to 44 (from 55) and start dropping from Act 4, while the Divine Orb is more common. The Omen of Corruption is no longer obtainable. Overall leveling crafting is more accessible earlier, but end-game crafting via recombination disappears.

## Martial Artist And Spirit Walker — Two New Ascendancies

**Martial Artist** is Monk's third ascendancy: a master of illusion, using runes carved into the body to create illusions, summon illusory bells to assist in combat, and fighting mainly with bare hands. This class meshes directly with the unique [Facebreaker](/mechanics/items/facebreaker) — gloves that let you use mace skills with bare hands. The patch buffs the combo foundation directly: :wiki-link{url="https://www.poe2wiki.net/wiki/Tempest_Bell"} now keeps a maximum of 3 bells active (from 1) and can be Ancestrally Boosted like a Strike skill, so the Monk bell route is noticeably stronger right from the start.

**Spirit Walker** is Huntress's second ascendancy: calling spirits in three directions — Stag, Owl, or Bear — taming fearsome beasts bound as companions, or summoning a spectral companion to fight alongside. The companion direction is deep enough to need its own note — see [Spirit Walker Companion Beast Hunt](/mechanics/spirit-walker-companion-beast-hunt). The patch buffs :wiki-link{url="https://www.poe2wiki.net/wiki/Tame_Beast"} from 40% more damage at Gem Level 9 up to 84% at Level 20, and summons the just-tamed Beast immediately if you have enough spirit, so the foundation of companion builds is much stronger.

## Player System Changes

A few player-side changes are big enough to reshape defence and damage calculations.

**Leech is rewritten.** Each resource type (Life, Mana, Energy Shield) has only one leech instance active at a time — the highest-recovery instance runs first, and only when it expires does the next take over. And there's a damage cap for leech: hits under 40,000 total damage are unaffected, but hits above that threshold count as only 40,000 damage for leech purposes. One-shot builds with huge hits no longer leech back to full from a single blow — leech becomes a steady sustain source rather than a burst, though increasing % leech still always increases the amount you receive.

**Energy Shield recharge is nerfed indirectly but broadly.** The small passive granting "Energy Shield Recharge Rate" is removed from the tree, replaced by "faster start of Energy Shield Recharge" at a lower value (15% down to 6%). A whole series of notables are converted from recharge rate to faster-start at smaller numbers, and the suffixes are cut deep — "of Buffering" from 36-40% down to 12-15%, "of Ardour" from 41-45% down to 16-19%, "of Suffusion" from 46-50% down to 20-23%. No line proudly says "ES nerf", but added together, builds relying on ES recharge to sustain get significantly weaker — the kind of nerf you only see by reading the whole Passive Tree section.

**Vaal Pact changes its nature.** The keystone now grants 50% more amount of Life Leeched and 67% less Life Leech speed, along with "Cannot Recover Life other than from Leech" and "Life Leech effects are not removed when Unreserved Life is Filled". It drops "Life Leech is Instant" and drops "Cannot use Life Flasks" — Vaal Pact becomes slow-but-large leech instead of the instant leech of before.

The rest are smaller changes worth knowing: the keyword **"Defences"** is dropped, every place that used it now spells out "Armour, Evasion and Energy Shield" — clarifying that these mods don't apply to Runic Ward, Resistance or Block. The chance to Deflect formula switches to a linear curve capped at 95%. Bleed on the player no longer increases damage while moving (the bleed you inflict on monsters is unchanged). And Armour and Evasion from items are buffed at high level — about 33% more at level 65, tapering to 15% at level 80+, while Energy Shield from items is about 8% more at level 65.

## Impact On Builds And Meta

Since the league isn't live yet, this section is reading numbers on paper — not yet a conclusion tested in-game.

**Infernal Legion takes a heavy nerf, directly affecting a build note I have.** :wiki-link{url="https://www.poe2wiki.net/wiki/Infernal_Legion"} III **is no longer obtainable**, and I and II are both halved: minions take 20% down to 10% of maximum Life as Fire Damage per second, and the ignite is also reduced from 20% to 10% of the Minion's Maximum Life as Base Fire Damage — the radius stays at 1.5m (I) / 2m (II), with II compensated by +20% Fire Resistance for minions. The [Dinomancer Lich Elephant](/builds/witch/dinomancer-lich-elephant) build relies on the ignite loop of Infernal Legion — losing tier III means losing a scaling tier, and the halved self-damage slows both the ignite engine and the Minion Instability loop. This needs re-evaluating early in week one of the league, not setting aside.

**Minion builds have both buffs and new tools.** Two new support gems, **Minion Splash** and **Minion Splash II**, give striking minions Melee Splash — opening up clear for melee minions. Tame Beast is heavily buffed (see above). The mechanic for removing minions when swapping weapon sets now prioritizes new minions over old ones, avoiding wasted ability cooldowns, and Minion Instability is fixed so it no longer bypasses Last Gasp. Added up, minion-summoners remain a strong archetype — only the Infernal Legion ignite branch needs caution.

**The unique pool expands, with a few worth watching.** :wiki-link{url="https://www.poe2wiki.net/wiki/Mageblood"} appears in the list of new Path of Exile 2 uniques — a belt that defined the entire endgame meta in Path of Exile 1, so its appearance here is a big signal. Loreweave and Geofri's Sanctuary are also familiar names from PoE1 returning. :wiki-link{url="https://www.poe2wiki.net/wiki/The_Hollow_Mask"} is reworked — it now grants the skill Wildwood's Gifts and 80-100% increased Reservation Efficiency of Remnant Skills. The two uniques revealed before launch are [The Auspex](/mechanics/items/the-auspex) (single-minion utility, Mist Raven) and [Facebreaker](/mechanics/items/facebreaker) (empty-hand mace, matching Martial Artist).

**League-start tooling is noticeably smoother.** Community creators can now make a `.build` file for players to download and load straight in the client — showing the recommended passives, ascendancy, skill gems, and support gems. The Fragment Stash Tab is finally usable in Path of Exile 2, and trade adds a quick-search via Shift-Alt Click on an item for an instant price-check (toggling each mod on/off to see its effect on price). Re-leveling is faster too: the campaign is shortened — the second Dreadnaught area is removed (the Act 2 boss moved into The Dreadnaught), Act 3 is reordered, the Waterways lever became a pressure pad, and monster density in the second half of the campaign is reduced.

## To Test When The League Goes Live

The league launches 29/05 — the points below aren't spelled out clearly enough in the patch notes, so I'll have to log them in-game:

- **The real numbers of Runic Ward** — what's the cap, what's the recovery speed, how exactly does the defence trade-off work on armour level 55+. Is this defence layer worth investing in or just a league gimmick.
- **Kalguuran Skills and Supports** — 21 skills + 8 supports crafted from the Remnant: what cost mechanic do they use, do they compete with normal gems, which ones are worth crafting early.
- **Infernal Legion minion build post-nerf** — re-measure the real ignite DPS of [Dinomancer Lich Elephant](/builds/witch/dinomancer-lich-elephant) after losing tier III; decide whether to pivot supports or change archetype.
- **Atlas Tree farming pace** — how many maps does completing the whole Fortress take to allocate the full tree; Atlas progression is now how-far-you-farm rather than optimize-points.
- **Masters of the Atlas** — which Doryani/Hilda/Jado nodes are worth picking for farming, since you swap freely each map, this is the strategy-optimization layer.
- **Crafting economy without Recombinator** — how do Expedition off + Recombinator removed push the price of end-game crafted items; can the Genesis Tree base types and Alloys fill the gap.
