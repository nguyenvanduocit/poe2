# Shield Wall - Warrior/Warbringer

---

### Identity

### Description

This build levels with **Boneshatter** + **Rolling Slam** until level 28, then transitions into **Shield Wall** once you can equip a strong armour-based shield base (notably **Rampart Shield** at that breakpoint).

The core scaling lever is simple: **Armour on your shield = damage**. You craft/upgrade shields aggressively as you level, and you solve resource sustain with **Mana Leech** (mandatory) and **Life Leech** on jewelry.

Warcries are non-negotiable: they provide defensive uptime, sustain, and damage amplification. **Warbringer** is the best campaign ascendancy because it makes warcry gameplay smooth and spammable.

### Pros & Cons

**Strengths**
- Very high single-target damage after level 28
- Extremely tanky (shield + block + warcry sustain)
- Clear, simple gearing priority (armour on shield)
- Campaign-friendly progression and easy swaps

**Weaknesses**
- Transition build: pre-28 feels different than post-28
- Requires leech on jewelry to comfortably spam Shield Wall
- Positioning matters (Shield Wall hit placement)

**Ascendancy Notes:** Warbringer is recommended for campaign/leveling. You can consider swapping to Titan or Smith later (e.g., after later major ascendancy milestones) once your gear and shield bases stabilize.

### Gem Setups

**Pre-28 (Acts 1 → 28): Main Damage**
```
Boneshatter
└─ (Support) Rapid Attacks OR Concentrated Effect
```

**Pre-28 Utility / Setup**
```
Rolling Slam
└─ (Support) Rapid Attacks OR Concentrated Effect
```
- Keep Rolling Slam’s damage low enough to reliably set up stuns (don’t overlevel it if it starts killing targets before the setup matters).

**Warcries (Core)**
- Infernal Cry: pack clear via explosions

**Post-28 (Main Setup): Shield Wall**
```
Shield Wall
├─ Brutality
├─ Melee Physical Damage
└─ Rapid Attacks (swap to Pulverise/Concentrated Effect for bosses)
```
- Shield Wall doesn’t “overlap” well; aim so the shield edge connects for consistent damage.

**Post-28 Warcries (Very Important)**
- Fortifying Cry: sustain + defence
- Seismic Cry: stun + damage push
- Infernal Cry: remains best for pack pops

**Movement**
- Shield Charge: core mobility and block activation while moving

## Gear Roadmap

### Phase 1: Campaign Start (Act 1 → Level 28)

**Priority Order:**
1. **Weapon:** high physical damage **Mace/Hammer** (dual-wield early if it’s stronger)
2. **Resists/Life:** basic survivability, don’t over-optimize early
3. **Utility:** buy/sell gems for gold if you’re starved early

### Phase 2: Transition (Level 28+)

**Build-Defining Gear:**
- **Shield (MOST IMPORTANT):** armour as high as possible
  - Best base at the breakpoint: **Rampart Shield**
  - Craft for **flat armour** and/or **% armour** whenever you upgrade bases
- **Jewelry (MANDATORY):**
  - **Mana Leech** (required)
  - **Life Leech** (strongly recommended)

**Weapons:**
- 1H Mace/Hammer with high physical damage; +levels to melee skills is a premium roll.

## Leveling Path

### Acts Strategy (Step-by-step)

**Stage 1 (Act 1 → Level 28):**
- Main: Boneshatter
- Setup: Rolling Slam (stun priming; don’t overlevel if it breaks your setup)
- Warcry: Infernal Cry for pack pops

**Stage 2 (Level 28+): Switch to Shield Wall**
- Equip a high-armour shield base (Rampart Shield is the early standout)
- Swap main skill to Shield Wall and add the warcry package (Fortifying/Seismic/Infernal)
- Add Shield Charge for movement and block uptime

## FAQ & Resources

### Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: When do I swap to Shield Wall?**
A: Immediately at **level 28** once you can equip a strong armour-based shield (Rampart Shield is the ideal base at that point).

**Q: Why is Mana Leech mandatory?**
A: Shield Wall’s mana cost is high enough that without Mana Leech you can’t reliably spam the skill.

**Q: What’s the #1 gear stat?**
A: **Armour on your shield**. Treat shield upgrades like weapon upgrades.
